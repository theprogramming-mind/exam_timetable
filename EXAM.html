The SPARK Method — built for the lazy reader
Every single study session in this timetable follows this exact 5-step process. No guessing what to do next.
✅ Why this works even when you don't feel like reading: SPARK removes all decision-making from your study session. You never ask "what should I do now?" — the next step is always defined. Even on your worst motivation day, you can follow a checklist.
S
Scan first — never read blind
⏱ 10–15 minutes
Before reading a single word — flip through the entire topic. Read only headings, subheadings, bold words, diagrams, and summary boxes. Your brain needs a skeleton before it can hang flesh on it. After scanning, ask yourself: "What is this topic actually about?" Write one sentence answer. Now you're ready to read.
P
Pomodoro read — 25 minutes on, 5 off
⏱ 25 min read · 5 min recall write
Read one sub-topic at a time in 25-minute blocks. At the end of each 25 minutes — close the notes. Do NOT re-read. Instead, on a blank page, write: the 3 most important things you just read. No peeking. This forces your brain to process, not just scan. Then take a 5-minute break. Repeat for the next sub-topic.
A
Active dump — everything from memory
⏱ 20 minutes
After finishing a full topic (all sub-topics done) — close every note. On a blank page, write everything you can remember about the entire topic. No hints, no peeking. Just everything your brain can produce. After the dump, open your notes and circle only the things you missed or got wrong. Those circles are your weak spots — review them specifically next time.
R
Reduce to a card — compress everything
⏱ 15 minutes
Take one blank piece of paper (or phone note). Write the topic name at the top. Underneath, write only the most important points — maximum 10 bullet points. Short. Dense. No sentences unless needed. This is your Summary Card. You will use it every morning and before every exam. The card is the product of each day's study. Guard it.
K
Knock out past questions
⏱ 45–60 minutes
Attempt past questions related to today's topic. Set a real timer. No notes open during attempt. After the timer ends — mark every answer honestly. For every wrong answer: go back to your notes, find the correct answer, and write a correction note on your summary card. Past questions don't just test you — they teach you the exact format the examiners use. Never skip this step.

🚫 Hard rules — non-negotiable
1.Phone on full Do Not Disturb during every focus block — not silent, full DND. Notifications kill deep focus even when you don't respond to them.
2.No new material the morning of an exam. Summary card recap only. Your job that morning is to access what you know — not panic-load new information.
3.Always mark past questions the same day you attempt them. Mistakes not reviewed within 24 hours are effectively not corrected.
4.Never leave a mock test unmarked. Attempting without marking is entertainment, not studying.
5.No studying past 10pm. The marginal gain from one more hour is less than the loss from impaired sleep.
6.Rest days during the prep phase (none scheduled) are the only days with reduced load. Free days during exam phase are intensive review days — not rest days.
